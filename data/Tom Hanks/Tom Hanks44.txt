As the players catch wind of the elements of the party, each adds a little monkey wrench so that one set of hookers ends up giving demos at the bride's shower, the brides friends end up dressed as hookers in a room with a number of non English speaking Japanese businessmen, and so on, as things get out of hand.                <em class="nobr">Written by

                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Works as an amusing time-passer thanks to a likable debut performance by Tom Hanks</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="6" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        29 April 2005 | by <a href="/user/ur1173088/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">MovieAddict2016</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2005-04-29" />
                              (UK)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur1173088/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">&quot;Bachelor Party&quot; is very much like other films of its genre except that it is one of Tom Hanks' very early efforts and, as a result, is more fondly remembered than it probably would be without Hanks.<br /><br />Yes, before Hanks turned into Forrest Gump and an Oscar-winning heavyweight actor, he desperately starred in a 1984 teen sex comedy not unlike &quot;Porky's,&quot; &quot;The Last American Virgin&quot; or &quot;American Pie.&quot; &quot;Splash&quot; had been a success by now so the marketers could rely upon his newly-created status to promote &quot;Bachelor Party,&quot; and it paid off -- this movie was an unexpected hit when it came out (albeit a small one).<br /><br />Tom Hanks is a fine actor but I have to say that he had a certain charm about his acting that made many mediocre '80s comedies -- including &quot;Turner and Hooch,&quot; &quot;The 'burbs&quot; and of course &quot;Bachelor Party&quot; -- immensely likable despite their flaws.<br /><br />Yes, this is a crude, lewd, rude movie but it has its fair share of embarrassing moments, laugh-out-loud segments and cheesy (but fun) segments. Good entertainment for a Friday or Saturday night, and a good glimpse at a much younger, thinner, more comedic (and slapstick) Tom Hanks before he got all weepy-eyed and serious on us.</p>
