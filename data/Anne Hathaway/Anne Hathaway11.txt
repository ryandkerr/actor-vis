        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="1/10">
                        <div style="width: 10px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Valentine&#x27;s Day: 23 actors, zero stars</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="1" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        15 February 2010 | by <a href="/user/ur3581954/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">Mark Braun (mbraun@ameritech.net)</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2010-02-15" />
                              (Norridge, IL)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur3581954/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">Ever sat through 120 minutes of Tasters Choice commercials in a row?<br /><br />WE bought the VIP seating and dinner for this god-awful mess. Started at 7:18 and when I checked my watch at 8:30, my wife asked me how long it was. &#x22;2 hours, 23 minutes&#x22; I said. She was not amused... and neither were the couple of couples who existed at 9 pm in front of us. <br /><br />Tepid, shop-worn cuts of lives that criss-cross each other, this worked for Altman much of the time but not here. Titles ran for way, way too long and cardboard direction (i.e. silent people holding conversations behind actors) was too dumb to be believed. The humor was lightweight when it happened at all, and we, the audience always felt like there&#x27;s got to be a payoff somewhere. Editing was sloppy when not entirely flat. It was as if we&#x27;d just missed the direction: &#x22;aaaand walk,&#x22; over and over again.<br /><br />I have a rule that only a true piece of junk uses gag reels for end titles, and sadly, this did, too. In fact, the best line in the film wasn&#x27;t really in the film at all, but out of Julia Roberts&#x27; mouth as reference to PRETTY WOMAN which was a gem. If anything, this stands as a mess worthy f $1 rental just to see how bad it can get.</p>
