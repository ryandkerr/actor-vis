        <h2>Storyline</h2>
        
        <div class="inline canwrap" itemprop="description">
            <p>
Based on Gail Carson Levine's award winning novel, this is the story of Ella, a young woman who was given a "gift" of obedience by a fairy named Lucinda. She must obey anything anyone tells her to do. When her mother passes away, she is cared for by her thoughtless and greedy father who remarries a loathsome woman with two treacherous daughters. This modern-day, fantasy Cinderella story features fairies, ogres and elves...as well as a hero in the guise of Prince Charmont, whom Ella falls in love with. Unlike Cinderella though, she must depend on herself and her intelligence to get her through her troubles and find Lucinda in order for her "curse" to be broken!                <em class="nobr">Written by
<a href="/search/title?plot_author=M.+C.+Gomez&view=simple&sort=alpha&ref_=tt_stry_pl"
>M. C. Gomez</a></em>            </p>

        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">

                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Surprisingly cute</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="7" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        14 July 2009 | by <a href="/user/ur0111731/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">smatysia (feldene@comcast.net)</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2009-07-14" />
                              (Houston)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur0111731/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">It seems that many, many commentators disliked this movie because it wasn&#x27;t at all like the book. Fair enough, I suppose, but movies seldom are. In any case, I haven&#x27;t read that book, and was blissfully unaware that this film was supposedly adapted from one. I found it surprisingly cute. Many of the campy things hit the mark, like the &#x22;medeival modern&#x22; anachronistic setting, reminiscent of The Flintstones cartoon wherein it was filled with modern things constructed from prehistoric materials; and the modern songs transposed to the time setting herein. That last was a bit like Moulin Rouge, which annoyed me at first, but then grew on me. I don&#x27;t believe that I&#x27;ve seen this Anne Hathaway before, but she is intriguing. Her vivacity and wide smile remind me a bit of Julia Roberts. Can&#x27;t say that I was much impressed with Hugh Dancy or Aiden McArdle. Minnie Driver was most charming as the incompetent fairy, and Vivica Fox was quite amusing as the lush fairy. It&#x27;s too bad that Parminder Nagra&#x27;s part was so small, as she always lights up a screen. And Cary Elwes couldn&#x27;t hide his amusement with the material as he munched on the scenery. I say check this out, if you aren&#x27;t going to throw a fit over the book.</p>
