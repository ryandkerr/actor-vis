                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">You don&#39;t have to like cats to enjoy The Cat Returns</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />

                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">&quot;The Cat Returns&quot; is a beautiful film as one would expect from Studio Ghibli. It is probably their lightest film to date and certainly the shortest of the ones available on DVD in the west. I don&#39;t think this is a fault though sometimes that is just what I want in a film.<br /><br />The plot suggests that it is the sort of film that would only appeal to children: A girl rescues a cat who turns out to be a prince among cats, in return she is invited to marry him and turns to The Cat Business Office for help. While it is obviously ideal for children, it is rated U by the BBFC with the customer advice that it &quot;Contains mild peril&quot; it is also a lot of fun for the older viewer, I laughed out loud several times when I watched it.<br /><br />People who enjoyed &quot;Whisper of the Heart&quot; may be pleased to see the return of the Baron in a much larger role.<br /><br />This is director Hiroyuki Morita&#39;s only film to date but on the strength of this I&#39;m hoping he will be directing further Studio Ghibli projects.</p>
