                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Sammy Davis Jr.&#x27;s A Man Called Adam was a fine dramatic showcase for him</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="9" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        3 March 2014 | by <a href="/user/ur11228318/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">tavm</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2014-03-03" />
                              (Baton Rouge, La.)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur11228318/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">Just watched this on Netflix disc with my mom. We both enjoyed this obscure film from 1966 about a jazz musician who suffers ups and downs because of a past he can&#x27;t escape. Sammy Davis Jr. is Adam Johnson, a trumpeter with a short temper and a messed up social life. Ossie Davis is his club owner friend Nelson, Frank Sinatra Jr. is his Caucasion prot&#xE9;g&#xE9; Vincent, Cicely Tyson is his activist lover Claudia, Louis Armstrong is her grandfather who&#x27;s also a jazz musician named Willie, and Peter Lawford is his agent Manny. All are very compelling in their roles which takes on many issues with racism and why Adam feels the way he does. Also interesting seeing such up-and-coming stars like Ja&#x27;net Du Bois, Johnny Brown (both eventually of &#x22;Good Times&#x22;), and Lola Falana in some roles. Oh, and the director is Leo Penn, father of Sean Penn. So on that note, I highly recommend A Man Called Adam. Oh, and I also loved Mel Torme doing his thing in one number.</p>
