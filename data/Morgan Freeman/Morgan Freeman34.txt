    <div class="article" id="titleUserReviewsTeaser">
        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="9/10">
                        <div style="width: 90px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">A Brutal-Yet-Touching Film</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="9" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        15 April 2007 | by <a href="/user/ur4445210/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">ccthemovieman-1</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2007-04-15" />
                              (United States)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur4445210/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">For a martial arts picture, &quot;Unleashed&quot; was above average because it had some heart and soul to it.....not just people beating the crap out of one another for two straight hours.<br /><br />However, be wary of those who claim this isn&#39;t an action film. That&#39;s not true: there is a lot of action, quite a bit of brutality and profanity, mostly by Bob Hoskins&#39; character &quot;Bart&quot; in this movie. Hoskins plays profane, nasty slave-owner who has locked up and trained &quot;Danny&quot; (Jet Li) to be the ultimate fighting machine. Once Danny is &quot;unleashed&quot; (literally) he&#39;s a killer and helps the gangster Bart in his criminal work.<br /><br />Then, in a moment of further greed, Hoskins agrees to enter his man in a death match with a lot of money at stake. That match turns out to be a farce with Danny winning in a matter of seconds. The other promoter wants a rematch, and with more entertainment. In the meantime, however, Danny escapes his captors and winds up at the house of an older blind American, &quot;Sam,&quot; played by Morgan Freeman. He and his 18-year-old daughter &quot;Natalie&quot; (Kelly Condon) befriend young Danny, slowly bringing him out of his &quot;animal&quot; state. Everyone discovers Danny has some very human characteristics and is basically a nice, gentle guy. The family helps uncover that through music (piano playing, to be exact). These are all touching scenes.<br /><br />But then - and this is almost a clich&eacute; in these kind of films - the bad guy comes back, snatches him away and his former bad life comes back....except Danny is a changed man. How he can get out of this latest predicament and what happens to this new &quot;adopted&quot; family, takes up the rest of the story. That last part has the same thing: ultra-violent and touching scenes.<br /><br />Jet Li does an excellent job in here, not only displaying his amazing physical talents but also showing us he has warmth in his face and heart.<br /><br />Overall: a brutal, yet touching film: an odd combination.</p>
                    </div>
