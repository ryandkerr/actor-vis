                    <strong itemprop="name">You won&#x27;t want it to end</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />

><span itemprop="author">jtindahouse</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2015-07-02" />
                              (Nelson, New Zealand)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur3793011/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">There are films that you just can&#x27;t wait to be over, then there are films that enjoyable but you&#x27;re still happy for them to finish. And finally there are films that you could happily have go on for days on end. This is a rare case of the latter, especially in the form of a sequel. Is it better than the original? That&#x27;s a hard question to answer because both are absolutely hilarious throughout and to separate two films based purely on how much you laughed is a tough thing to do. That&#x27;s the beauty of anything with Seth MacFarlane&#x27;s name on it, his sole objective is just to make you have a good time. It just so happens he&#x27;s good enough at filmmaking to actually make a pretty decent film at the same time.<br /><br />The two hour runtime just flies by. There&#x27;s only perhaps two dull scenes and both are forgivable as they&#x27;re purely setting up crucial plot points. Aside from that every scene is a riot. The cameos are plentiful (the cast on paper is actually quite stunning when you consider some of the names they managed to get - a sure sign that people simply want to work with MacFarlane) and all are given brilliant material to work with. Liam Neeson&#x27;s was probably the best of the bunch for me, followed closely by Tom Brady&#x27;s. <br /><br />Having no Mila Kunis doesn&#x27;t overly hurt the film as Amanda Seyfried does a great job replacing her in the lead female role. Mark Wahlberg is back thankfully and his chemistry with Ted is equally as good as the first. There really isn&#x27;t any negatives I can think of which is a pretty amazing thing to say. If you enjoyed the first, you&#x27;ll love this one too. If you didn&#x27;t like the first, well to borrow a line from Peter Griffin, &#x22;chances are I do not care for you as a person&#x22; (and you can probably go ahead and ignore everything written in this review).</p>
