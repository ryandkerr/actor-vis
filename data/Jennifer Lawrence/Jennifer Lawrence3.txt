        <h2>Storyline</h2>
        
        <div class="inline canwrap" itemprop="description">
            <p>
A fictional film set in the alluring world of one of the most stunning scandals to rock our nation, American Hustle tells the story of brilliant con man Irving Rosenfeld (<a href="/name/nm0000288?ref_=tt_stry_pl">Christian Bale</a>), who along with his equally cunning and seductive British partner Sydney Prosser (<a href="/name/nm0010736?ref_=tt_stry_pl">Amy Adams</a>) is forced to work for a wild FBI agent Richie DiMaso (<a href="/name/nm0177896?ref_=tt_stry_pl">Bradley Cooper</a>). DiMaso pushes them into a world of Jersey powerbrokers and mafia that's as dangerous as it is enchanting. <a href="/name/nm0719637?ref_=tt_stry_pl">Jeremy Renner</a> is Carmine Polito, the passionate, volatile, New Jersey political operator caught between the con-artists and Feds. Irving's unpredictable wife Rosalyn (<a href="/name/nm2225369?ref_=tt_stry_pl">Jennifer Lawrence</a>) could be the one to pull the thread that brings the entire world crashing down.                <em class="nobr">Written by

        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="1/10">
                        <div style="width: 10px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Don&#x27;t get conned by the hype.</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="1" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        28 December 2013 | by <a href="/user/ur3597668/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">Chris</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2013-12-28" />
                              (United States)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur3597668/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">I could not wait for this movie to end. It was a complete atrocity. The overly-indulgent, sloppy chaos amounted to nothing in terms of character or story. A vague lesson spoon-fed to the audience during the first 20 minutes is I&#x27;m guessing what is supposed to excuse the senseless and bumbling bore of a circus for the next 100.<br /><br />I absolutely do not understand the praise of the acting in this film. I never bought into the actors as their roles, though, honestly, there was not much else holding it together for me. Was there a script? Was there a director??? The entire sad excuse for a film seemed to be the improvised work of the actors themselves contrived in a garage somewhere after a long night of drinking.<br /><br />I never was really motivated to read into the movie, and I was never really fully engaged or entertained by the shallow antics, long-hanging scenes that go on way too long, or 70&#x27;s retro-obsession. I&#x27;m not sure if I&#x27;m supposed to be mesmerized by watching makeup applied to Christian Bale&#x27;s head or how cool walking into a 70&#x27;s themed wardrobe would be. It seemed none of the actors could keep their accents. I would never label this as a comedy-- I did laugh from time to time because of how badly I wanted it to be over.<br /><br />For a respectable story about individuals trying to reinvent themselves please see The Great Gatsby, The Departed, or The Town (and I&#x27;m sure there are many more).</p>
