        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="6/10">
                        <div style="width: 60px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">A Two Hour Preview of What to Expect in the Final Movie.</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="6" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        19 November 2014 | by <a href="/user/ur37599117/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">prospectus_capricornium</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2014-11-19" />
                              (Philippines)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur37599117/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">You may want to save your money and time seeing this movie and wait for the last installment to come out next year, instead. Aside from Jennifer Lawrence, this movie offers nothing more than just a long and tiring tale of preparation for an impending rebellion.<br /><br />Readers shouldn&#x27;t get surprised: Mockingjay&#x27;s first half chronicles District 13&#x27;s efforts to groom Katniss as their heroine against the ruthless ruling Capitol. The process of transforming her into what they call the &#x27;Mockingjay&#x27;, a symbol of revolution, is engaging enough to ignite interest, but only up to a point, when long speeches and depictions of what the Hunger Games&#x27; downfall has caused, have almost become the only few things eating up the biggest slice of the movie&#x27;s airtime pie. There is nothing much to see here, and while its refreshing to finally see the landscapes to have finally shifted away from the arenas, lack of action eventually makes the scenes that are almost only showing the events in the confinements, hard to appreciate, not to mention difficult to extract sense from.<br /><br />Fortunately, it has Jennifer Lawrence, and that alone, is enough to pull the entire movie away whenever it leans toward making the audience fall to sleep. Jennifer&#x27;s charm proves to be the biggest asset of this movie, and she alone, singularly saves the film from being a total nonsense. Toward the end, it isn&#x27;t clear whether the makers of the film have made their point (aside from making more money of course) in splitting the book into two movies. This first half only presents a preview of what to happen next in the other half, only stretched into two hours of tiring sameness and boring exchanges of bland dialogues. The new characters are interesting yeah, but I&#x27;m not sure if anyone of them has offered something more than Lawrence, to spark further interest. Well, at least we can take comfort from the fact that the last movie next year will certainly offer a lot more, gripping action and drama, and that&#x27;s really something to look forward to.<br /><br />Mockingjay Pt.1 falls below the heights reached by the previous two films. Minus Lawrence, this film is a real bore, and its hard to seek something more than her to recommend. I give this film 6 of my 10 stars.</p>
