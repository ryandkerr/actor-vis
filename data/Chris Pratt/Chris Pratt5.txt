        <h2>Storyline</h2>
        
        <div class="inline canwrap" itemprop="description">
            <p>
Theodore is a lonely man in the final stages of his divorce. When he's not working as a letter writer, his down time is spent playing video games and occasionally hanging out with friends. He decides to purchase the new OS1, which is advertised as the world's first artificially intelligent operating system, "It's not just an operating system, it's a consciousness," the ad states. Theodore quickly finds himself drawn in with Samantha, the voice behind his OS1. As they start spending time together they grow closer and closer and eventually find themselves in love. Having fallen in love with his OS, Theodore finds himself dealing with feelings of both great joy and doubt. As an OS, Samantha has powerful intelligence that she uses to help Theodore in ways others hadn't, but how does she help him deal with his inner conflict of being in love with an OS?                <em class="nobr">Written by

                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">I saw Her this weekend, and it&#x27;s my favorite movie of the year. Everything about it was fantastic: the acting, the directing, the writing. It could&#x27;ve easily been a clich&#xE9;d look at technology or the future, but it&#x27;s fundamentally a very human story about evolution. It&#x27;s about having to deal with unfulfillment and things like divorce and loneliness, but it&#x27;s also undercut with hope, whether it be through Theo&#x27;s relationships with Samantha (Johansson) and Amy (Adams) or even the stylistic touches, like having Theo&#x27;s red clothes set against a muted Los Angeles background. There are parallels drawn between all the characters&#x27; journeys without it feeling contrived, and even Theo&#x27;s job--writing letters for other people--represents his relationship with his OS; on the surface, they may seem like &#x22;just letters&#x22; or &#x22;just a computer&#x22;, but they&#x27;re outlets for his emotions and desires.<br /><br />Oh, and everyone in the cast is phenomenal; it&#x27;s a shame Johansson is &#x22;ineligible&#x22; for an award. BS. She&#x27;s fantastic, and this is an even tougher role to pull off.<br /><br />This is a story about life, and it&#x27;s perfect.<br /><br />A.<br /><br />Full review: http://polarbearstv.com/2013/12/30/her-review/</p>
