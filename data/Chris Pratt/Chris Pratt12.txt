        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="5/10">
                        <div style="width: 50px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">This came from the pen of an Oscar winner?</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="5" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        11 December 2009 | by <a href="/user/ur0945066/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">BA_Harrison</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2009-12-11" />
                              (Hampshire, England)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur0945066/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">I appreciate all types of horror, from the intelligent and atmospheric to the plain dumb and gory, but in the case of Jennifer&#x27;s Body, I have to admit that I just don&#x27;t get it: I can&#x27;t identify with the characters; I fail to understand their motivation; I don&#x27;t appreciate their idiotic &#x27;hip&#x27; language; but most of all, I just cannot comprehend what the film was trying to be. Did writer Diablo Cody intend Jennifer&#x27;s Body to be a straight-up horror, a dark comedy, a teen drama, a pro-feminist tale, an allegory, or even perhaps, a mixture of all of these? Whatever the intention, it doesn&#x27;t really work.<br /><br />Veering awkwardly from one poorly constructed scene to another, Jennifer&#x27;s Body offers very little in the way of scares, laughs, or emotional content, and if there is a deeper meaning to the film&#x97;and the inclusion of recurring imagery does support this idea&#x97;then it&#x27;s virtually impossible to decipher, making it all rather redundant. Even those who choose to watch in the hope of seeing Megan Fox in the altogether will be disappointed: despite playing a sex-mad schoolgirl slut who becomes a seductive boy-eating succubus, she fails to flash the goods.<br /><br />Of course, there is the much touted lesbian clinch with nerdy best friend Needy (played by Amanda Seyfried, who is much hotter than Fox in my opinion) to keep the lads (or ladies) momentarily happy, but a little girl-on-girl action alone does not make a good film.</p>
