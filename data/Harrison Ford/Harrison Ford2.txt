Having left San Diego for New York City, Ron Burgundy is living the high life with his wife Veronica Corningstone and son Walter Burgundy. However, when the boss decides to promote Veronica to full time lead anchor and fire Ron, everything changes. Now heading back to San Diego, Ron is washed up and working part time at Sea World. His shot at redemption though comes in the form of a man named Freddie Schapp, who's an executive producer at the Global News Network, the world's first 24 hour round the clock news channel. He hires Ron, who proceeds to reunite the news team of Champ, Brick, and Brian, and head back to New York City. While there Ron and his news team are given the graveyard shift and a challenge. Ron comes up with a radical new idea to transform the news and that puts him at the top of the game once again. But how long will Ron's newfound fame last?                 <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">Just awful...</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />

                        8 January 2014 | by <a href="/user/ur28798960/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">leonhart35</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2014-01-08" />
                              (United Kingdom)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur28798960/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">Of all the things I expected to feel when I walked out of the cinema after seeing this film, disgusted that I paid money to watch it was not one of them. <br /><br />No effort has been made to actually make this film any good, or any actual sequel to the first. The writers have just crammed it with as much stuff as they could that made the first film so brilliant, and all they succeeded in doing was making it an overblown, awkward film that leaves you knowing that during every seen they&#x27;re just trying to force a laugh out of you. Its also been a long time that I was so consciously aware that the actors are just performing lines for a camera, there&#x27;s nothing natural about any of them. It all feels painfully scripted, all the one liners blatantly placed just to drag a laugh kicking and screaming from your lips. <br /><br />The last 45 minutes goes off on such a bizarre tangent I honestly had no idea what the hell was going on or what the purpose of any of it was. <br /><br />Truly disappointed with this film, and incredibly annoyed by the knowledge that it&#x27;ll be a box office smash purely because the first one is such a legendary film.</p>
