        <h2>Did You Know?</h2>
    <div id="trivia" class="txt-block">
        <h4>Trivia</h4>


The two tropical island comedies, <a href="/title/tt0090297?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Water</a> (1985) and <a href="/title/tt0090856?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Club Paradise</a> (1986), shared a number of commonalities. Both featured settings that were British Principalities in the Carribean with at least one pompous English government official. Both had reggae connections, <a href="/name/nm0002490?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Bob Marley</a> in <a href="/title/tt0090297?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Water</a> (1985) and <a href="/name/nm0166580?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Jimmy Cliff</a> in <a href="/title/tt0090856?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Club Paradise</a> (1986). <a href="/title/tt0090297?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Water</a> (1985) was an English movie and co-starred an American blonde, <a href="/name/nm0882853?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Brenda Vaccaro</a>, whereas <a href="/title/tt0090856?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Club Paradise</a> (1986) was an American movie which co-starred an English blonde, <a href="/name/nm0493341?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Twiggy</a>. <a href="/name/nm0000564?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Peter O'Toole</a> is seen in <a href="/title/tt0090856?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Club Paradise</a> (1986) in a 'Cocked Hat' just as 'Michael Caine' is in <a href="/title/tt0090297?ref_=tt_trv_trv">Water</a> (1985). Both films were released in the USA within three months of each other.        <a href="trivia?ref_=tt_trv_trv"
class="nobr" >See more</a>  &raquo;
    </div>

                        <p itemprop="reviewBody"> I am quite disappointed with the average for this film.  I found it to be one of the funniest films I have ever seen.  The cast is superb and the script, although not one of the best written, is filled with humor that was was made even better by the acting.  It was filmed on the island of St. Lucia.  It's a story of a company drilling for oil and accidently discovers mineral water, a substance that is even more valuable than oil.  But it is important to keep it a secret.  Jimmie Walker, who I don't particularly like, plays the part of a radio dj and does it wonderfully.  Michael Caine and Brenda Vaccaro are fabulous as is everyone else.  Please give this film a chance.  I think it might be one of those little surprises that come along every once in a while.</p>
