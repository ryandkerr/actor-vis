                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">I went to see this movie without catching any reviews, expecting something rather depressing and underfunded.<br /><br />Let me stop there and start again.<br /><br />This movie is a revelation from start to finish. A convincing future world, deftly conveyed with so many subtle signals that I&#39;m sure it will benefit from further viewings. A completely &quot;other&quot; England which I was amazed to see realised in such detail. Clive Owen FINALLY has the heroic role we have been waiting for and is brilliant in it. Julianne Moore simply glows and I&#39;ve never enjoyed Sir Michael Caine so much before. The soundtrack is beautifully eclectic. Aside from some excellent classical choices, there&#39;s an evocative and alternative Spanish take on &quot;Ruby Tuesday&quot; which is a signature on the film. Wait during the end titles to enjoy an excremental song from Jarvis Cocker.<br /><br />The movie grabbed my attention right from the start, and never let go. Initially, it&#39;s the differences of this future world that intrigue. Then, when the action starts, what I found really surprising was the freshness of direction that made me react to bullets and violence as if I&#39;d never seen them in a movie before. If the script wasn&#39;t so wonderfully leavened with wit, it would be a grim and scary movie at times.<br /><br />Finally, the whole thing is lit brilliantly, from the authentic dim English days to the atmospheric ending.<br /><br />One to watch alongside &quot;The Handmaid&#39;s Tale&quot; some time....</p>
