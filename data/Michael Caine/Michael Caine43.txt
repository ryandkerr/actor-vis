        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">'hit' rather than 'eye' in the title would be better...</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />

                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        30 October 2002 | by <a href="/user/ur1485918/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">hugh1971</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2002-10-30" />
                              (London)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur1485918/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody"> Oh dear. I'm a big fan of Mr Caine and Mr Moore, and to be honest those two in the lead roles are the only reason to watch the film. Anyone lesser would have made it an utter waste of time. <br /><br />The film is hackneyed with an incomprehensible plot. Films based on 'doubles' are always dodgy, so much so that even in the 30s it was considered bad plotting to use them in detective stories. At some points in the film I just didn't know who was meant to be whom, and by the time of the second 'double cross' I just lost interest. <br /><br />While Caine and Moore were at times hilarious ('I come from a broken home...')a lot of the jokes and effects made me cringe. The scene where the train porter gets his head blown off had me rewinding to see if my eyes had not deceived me. That has to be the worst special effect for many years!<br /><br /> I also found the very obvious pitching of the film to the American audience patronising in the extreme. Tourist shots of London, Highland Games, stately homes, stuffy clubs, 'punk' taxi drivers and an unconvincing portrayal of the Queen - all this type of thing was being done far better and with greater irony by the Comic Strip team years before. <br /><br />So don't expect a great plot or gags but if you like Caine and Moore, it's worth watching - just. </p>
