    <div class="article" id="titleUserReviewsTeaser">
        <h2>User Reviews</h2>
        <div class="user-comments">
                    <div class="tinystarbar" title="10/10">
                        <div style="width: 100px;">&nbsp;</div>
                    </div>
                <span itemprop="review" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">  
                    <strong itemprop="name">One Of The Funniest And Most Violent Action Films In Years</strong>
                    <span itemprop="reviewRating" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
                        <meta itemprop="worstRating" content = "1" />
                        <meta itemprop="ratingValue" content="10" />
                        <meta itemprop="bestRating" content="10" />
                    </span>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        26 December 2014 | by <a href="/user/ur53933153/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span itemprop="author">john schneider</span></a>
                        <meta itemprop="datePublished" content="2014-12-26" />
                              (Austin, Texas)
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur53933153/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p itemprop="reviewBody">I&#x27;m going to be honest about this: I like unabashedly violent action films. I&#x27;m 42 years old, I&#x27;m from Texas and I can take it. Growing up with films like &#x27;Rambo&#x27;, &#x27;Die Hard&#x27;, &#x27;The Terminator&#x27; and &#x27;Robocop&#x27;, I can&#x27;t stand that watered-down &#x22;no-blood-no-swearing&#x22; gutless kiddie fare that is being served as &#x22;action&#x22; these days. I know the real thing when I see it - we used to see it all the time back in the day - and I hardly ever see it anymore.<br /><br />Which brings me to this movie. &#x27;Kingsman: The Secret Service&#x27; was being shown just around the corner from where I live, and because I loved two of director Matthew Vaughn&#x27;s previous films, &#x27;Layer Cake&#x27; and &#x27;Kick-Ass&#x27;, I naturally took the chance to see it. I had already read some comments from previous screenings that this film was totally bad-ass, and I can now say that&#x27;s actually putting it mildly. This film is nothing short of bat-sh&#xEE;t crazy.<br /><br />Years ago Tarantino said in an interview that he had written a James Bond script and that he would love to direct a Bond film. Sadly, that never happened, but ever since I read that I wondered what an R-rated Bond might be like. I don&#x27;t know, maybe Matthew Vaughn has read that interview too and saw the potential, because &#x27;Kingsman&#x27; is pretty much that: An ultra violent, funny, crazy, foul-mouthed James Bond film (with a little bit of &#x27;Men in Black&#x27; and &#x27;Mission Impossible&#x27; thrown in). You could say that this is to Bond what &#x27;Game of Thrones&#x27; is to &#x27;Lord of the Rings&#x27;: Where the former can&#x27;t and dare not go (for marketing and box office reasons), the latter joyfully and gloriously ventures. Dirty and (very black) humor - check. Bad language - check. Gratuitous violence - check. Needless to say, I was thrilled.<br /><br />But it&#x27;s also a fantastic action film with an amazing cast (Oscar winners Colin Firth and Michael Caine, plus Sam Jackson AND Mark Hamill) and spectacular, over-the-top fight-scenes that in some instances even rival films like &#x27;The Raid&#x27; for their sheer visceral intensity. In short, if you&#x27;re as fed up with lame wannabe Die-Hards and Terminators as I am, go watch this film. Apart from the rare &#x27;John Wick&#x27; or &#x27;Equalizer&#x27;, &#x27;Kingsman&#x27; seems to be pretty much the only antidote to the toothless, generic tripe Hollywood tries to pass for action these days. 9 stars for the film and the story, 11 stars for the balls to pull this off the way they did. Average = a perfect 10.<br /><br />EDIT:<br /><br />P.S. I recently stumbled upon an article on the importance of R rated movies. If you&#x27;re a film fan (especially of films that don&#x27;t cater to teenagers), you might find it as enlightening as I have:<br /><br />www.the-fanboy-perspective.com/the-importance-of-the-r-rating.html</p>
                    </div>
