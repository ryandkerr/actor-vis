                        <p itemprop="reviewBody"> A lot of people don't like this picture, but, I think it works. It is helmed by a strong cast of principle performers and carried off by a unique twist on the sequel impetus.  The idea is fairly original, as opposed to the usual: for this story, one would normally expect they'd just focus on another group of survivors and how they get out.  Instead of dealing with people wanting to get out, the plot is spurred on by people wanting to get IN.  Then, ironically, they become trapped and become the people who have to get out, along with some of those aforementioned survivors to keep with tradition.  I also have to give the film credit for that little bits of continuity, like Linda's body is still lying near the entrance of the ship the crew uses, the same hole the original movie's survivors escaped from.  In the end, the movie isn't as bad as it is often called.</p>
